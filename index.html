Mastering the Present Perfect

Connecting the past to the present seamlessly.

What is it?

The Present Perfect Tense is used to describe an action or state that happened at an indefinite time in the past, or began in the past and continues into the present.

💡 Remember: The exact time doesn't matter! If you say when it happened (e.g., yesterday, last year), use the Simple Past instead.

The Structure

Subject + Have / Has + Past Participle

Positive

I / You / We / They + have

He / She / It + has

"She has traveled to Rome."

Negative

Have + not = haven't

Has + not = hasn't

"We haven't finished yet."

Question

Have / Has goes to the front of the sentence.

"Have you seen my keys?"

Key Signal Words

These words are frequently used with the Present Perfect to give context about time:

Just

A very short time ago.

"I've just eaten breakfast."

Already

Sooner than expected.

"The train has already left."

Yet

Until now (used in negative/questions).

"Has he called you yet?"

Ever / Never

At any time / At no time (life experiences).

"Have you ever seen snow?"

Since / For

Since: specific point.
For: duration.

"Since 2015" / "For 5 years"
